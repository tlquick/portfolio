About Me

I am a Software Developer based in Sydney. I enjoy all types of coding: object oriented, functional, native, web and scripting. C++, Java, JavaScript or Python - they all have their place.

More About Me

I worked as a trainee software engineer for an accounting software company straight after graduating, rising to senior software engineer in under 3 years. I cut my teeth on C and C++, then started writing some Java and C#. Working on enterprise applications, with multiple executables sharing a central relational database. I loved designing and specifying new software, mentoring and supporting juniors as they found their feet.

After my twin daughters were born, I needed to step back from the cutting edge, so I took a part time gig as an IT lecturer (and subject coordinator) for a university pathway - teaching others the art of crafting good, clean, reusable code. I redirected my passion for programming into a passion for teaching students to program using Java, VB and more recently Python. I also taught shell script and networking using the IOS CLI to configure switches and routers.

I loved teaching the next generation of developers, they taught me a lot too. Every year I completed short courses and tutorials to keep a toe in with new technologies but my focus was always on researching emerging techniques in pedagogy particularly around teaching 1st year programming subjects. My programming courses had failure rates of 5-15% , compared to the typical 40-60% at other tertiary institutions. I won the Teaching Innovation Award for the College 3 times (2014, 2018, 2021) in recognition of the progressive practises I implemented in my programming subjects.

My philosophy has always been that anyone can learn to program, they just have to stick with it and practise. Student evaluations of my teaching were always around 3.8-4.2 out of 5, lots of 4s and 5s and a few 1s. Students either loved me or hated me - depending on how lazy they were ... LOL. I thought I would teach for the rest of my career, I got to talk about object oriented patterns, demonstrate techniques and discuss the intricacies of a programming solution.

Then COVID changed everything. Lectures were replaced with recordings, tutorials exercises too. All of a sudden I didn't get to teach or write code anymore. I still got to review student code but the spark was gone. I hated it. So I decided to upskill and go back to my first love. My kids are grown, so there is nothing stopping me now.

Since October 2022, I have completed a number of courses on DevOps and Spring Boot in Coursera and Udemy. I have taught myself React JS, NodeJS and ExpressJS as well, using MERN/PERN stacks. I have been implementing what I have learnt in GitHub, using small projects to test ideas and reaffirm knowledge.
Yes - I know its a big task and I have a lot to learn but I am committed to it. I am practising every day and I am getting exponentially better each time. My skills are mostly at Mid level at the moment.


TL;DR

I was a competent senior software engineer earlier in my career; a well-respected Programming Lecturer and I am committed to learning new skills to become competent in development again.
